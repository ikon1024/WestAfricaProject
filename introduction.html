0: Introduction

For thousands of years humans have lived in Africa, throughout most of the time there were little to no written records which made it difficult for historians to discover what life was like in the Early days of West Africa. The first people to write about Africa were Muslim scholars who began writing about the kingdom of Ghana in the 800's. During the time arounnd 500 and 1600 C.E., three kingdoms arose in West Africa. Ghana, Songhai, and Malli. By examining artifacts left by earlier cultures historians were provided helpful clues on how life was like in the past.

Several factors led to the development of early societys and kingdoms in West Africa. Over time villages grew into communities and traded with each other gaining resources that they couldn't have aquirred from their location and climate.
